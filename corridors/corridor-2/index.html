Corridor 02

Midline Anterior Skull Base

Overview

The midline anterior skull base corridor provides access to pathology from the cribriform plate through the planum sphenoidale, sella turcica, and upper clivus. This is the primary route for pituitary adenomas, craniopharyngiomas, tuberculum and planum meningiomas, Rathke cleft cysts, and clival chordomas. The corridor spans from the anterior cranial fossa floor to the posterior clinoids, with the cavernous sinuses defining the lateral boundaries.

Approaches
Endoscopic Endonasal Transsphenoidal
Standard sellar approach via nasal corridor and sphenoidotomy. Workhorse for pituitary adenomas, Rathke cleft cysts.
Extended Endonasal
Extends the sellar opening superiorly to planum or inferiorly to clivus. For craniopharyngiomas, tuberculum meningiomas, chordomas.
Subfrontal / Transbasal
Open craniotomy to the anterior skull base floor. For esthesioneuroblastomas, large anterior fossa meningiomas, or when endo is not feasible.

OR Pearls
Setup

Supine, head in Mayfield (open) or horseshoe (endonasal). Endonasal: 2-surgeon 4-hand technique, surgeon on right, 0° scope for sellar work transitioning to 30°/45° angled scopes for suprasellar and parasellar visualization. Image guidance registered to facial landmarks. Harvest nasoseptal flap early if CSF leak anticipated.

Decision Points
Endo vs Open?
Lateral extension beyond carotids, vascular encasement, prior surgery with scarred flap, or significant brain invasion may favor craniotomy.
Sphenoidotomy Extent
Standard sellar vs. extended (posterior ethmoidectomy + planum) vs. transclival — dictated by lesion epicenter and vertical extent.
Flap Strategy
Nasoseptal flap pedicled on posterior septal artery. Must harvest BEFORE posterior septectomy. Plan contralateral if septum involved.
Carotid Awareness
Identify paraclival and parasellar ICA segments. Lateral sellar limit = carotid prominence. Neuronavigation + micro-Doppler mandatory.
