Corridor 02

Midline Anterior Skull Base

Overview

The midline anterior skull base corridor provides access to pathology from the cribriform plate through the planum sphenoidale, sella turcica, and upper clivus. This is the primary route for pituitary adenomas, craniopharyngiomas, tuberculum and planum meningiomas, Rathke cleft cysts, and clival chordomas. The corridor spans from the anterior cranial fossa floor to the posterior clinoids, with the cavernous sinuses defining the lateral boundaries.

Approaches
Endoscopic Endonasal Transsphenoidal
Standard sellar approach via nasal corridor and sphenoidotomy. Workhorse for pituitary adenomas and Rathke cleft cysts.
Extended Endonasal
Extends the sellar opening superiorly to planum or inferiorly to clivus. For craniopharyngiomas, tuberculum meningiomas, and chordomas.
Subfrontal / Transbasal
Open craniotomy to the anterior skull base floor. For esthesioneuroblastomas, large anterior fossa meningiomas, or when endonasal is not feasible.

OR Pearls
Imaging Review

Sphenoid sinus pneumatization pattern (conchal, presellar, sellar). Septal deviation and nasal anatomy. ICA position and any bony deficiency over the carotid canal. Tumor relationship to the optic chiasm, including prefixed vs postfixed chiasm. Assess for cavernous sinus invasion.

Setup

Supine, head in Mayfield or horseshoe. Binostril technique with image guidance registered to facial landmarks. 0 degree scope for sellar work, transitioning to 30/45 degree angled scopes for suprasellar and parasellar visualization. Harvest nasoseptal flap early (before posterior septectomy) if skull base reconstruction will be needed.

Decision Points
Endo vs Open?
Lateral extension beyond the carotids, vascular encasement, or significant brain invasion may favor craniotomy. Prior surgery with a compromised nasoseptal flap is also a consideration.
Flap Strategy
Nasoseptal flap pedicled on the posterior septal artery. Must harvest before posterior septectomy. Plan contralateral flap if septum is involved by tumor.
Skull Base Reconstruction
Depends on defect size and whether there is a high-flow CSF leak. Options range from fat graft alone to multilayer closure with fascia lata graft buttressed by the nasoseptal flap. Synthetic options like TachoSil or Evarrest can supplement the repair.
Carotid Localization
Identify paraclival and parasellar ICA segments. Lateral sellar limit is the carotid prominence. Confirm position with neuronavigation and Doppler ultrasound.
