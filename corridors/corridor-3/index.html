Corridor 03

Middle Fossa & Petroclival

Overview

The middle fossa and petroclival corridor accesses pathology along the superior surface of the petrous bone, the floor of the middle cranial fossa, Meckel's cave, and the petroclival junction. This corridor is essential for petroclival meningiomas, trigeminal schwannomas, epidermoids extending into Meckel's cave, and temporal bone lesions including cholesteatoma and facial nerve decompression. The petrous apex, cavernous sinus, and tentorium define the surgical boundaries.

Approaches
Subtemporal / Kawase (Anterior Petrosectomy)
Temporal lobe elevation with drilling of Kawase's triangle to access the posterior fossa anterior to the IAC. For petroclival meningiomas, upper clival lesions, basilar trunk aneurysms.
Middle Fossa Approach
Extradural elevation of the temporal lobe to expose the tegmen, IAC, and petrous apex. For vestibular schwannomas with serviceable hearing, facial nerve decompression, tegmen defects.
Combined Petrosal (Transpetrosal)
Combines anterior (Kawase) and posterior petrosectomy with tentorial division. Maximum exposure of the petroclival region for large meningiomas spanning the tentorium.

OR Pearls
Setup

Supine, head in Mayfield, turned 60–90° contralateral for subtemporal. Temporal craniotomy flush to the middle fossa floor — the lower the bone flap, the less temporal lobe retraction. Lumbar drain to relax the brain. Extradural elevation of the temporal lobe identifies the GSPN, arcuate eminence, and tegmen tympani as key landmarks before any drilling.

Decision Points
Kawase vs Combined Petrosal?
Kawase alone for lesions anterior to the IAC. Add posterior petrosectomy and tentorial division for large petroclival meningiomas extending posterior to the IAC or below the tentorial edge.
Kawase Triangle Drilling
Bounded by V3, GSPN, and arcuate eminence. The petrous ICA is the anterior-medial limit. Must identify and preserve the cochlea (anteromedial to the arcuate eminence).
Hearing Preservation
Middle fossa approach preserves hearing by accessing the IAC from above without opening the labyrinth. Requires intraoperative ABR monitoring. Tumor must be <2cm with serviceable hearing.
Venous Considerations
Vein of Labbé limits posterior extent of temporal lobe retraction. Superior petrosal sinus divided during combined petrosal. Assess venous drainage preop — dominant Labbé is a contraindication to aggressive subtemporal retraction.
