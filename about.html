About This Project

Applied Neuroanatomy is a free, interactive neurosurgery education platform organized around surgical corridors. It combines cadaveric anatomy from the Rhoton Collection with radiology cases, key concepts, and operative pearls to support structured learning at every training level.

The platform is designed to support a flipped classroom model — residents review anatomy and concepts independently, then apply that knowledge in case-based discussions with attendings. Content progresses from foundational anatomy identification through imaging recognition to management concepts and operative decision-making.

Anatomy images are adapted from the Rhoton Cranial Anatomy and Surgical Approaches collection, used for educational purposes with attribution to Albert L. Rhoton Jr. and the Rhoton Collection.

Built by Shivani Baisiwala, MD with Claude as co-author
